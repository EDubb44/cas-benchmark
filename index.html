Client Advisory Services (CAS)

A Deep Dive into Industry Trends & Market Performance (2018-2024)

CAS: The Undisputed Growth Engine

Client Advisory Services (CAS) has unequivocally established itself as a primary driver of expansion within the accounting profession. The consistent, robust growth observed over the years underscores a fundamental market shift towards proactive, data-driven financial guidance.

Median CAS Practice Growth (All Respondents, 2024)

17%

This figure highlights the vitality and expanding demand for CAS, positioning it as a critical component for future-proofing accounting firms and ensuring their continued relevance and expansion.

Growing Contribution: CAS Revenue as % of Total Firm Revenue

This chart illustrates the increasing proportion of total firm revenue derived from Client Advisory Services for All Respondents. The upward trend signifies the growing strategic importance and financial contribution of CAS within accounting firms, moving from a smaller service line to a more substantial part of the overall business.

The steady rise in CAS as a percentage of total firm revenue underscores its evolution into a key revenue stream. This reflects successful practice development, increasing client adoption of CAS, and a strategic focus by firms on expanding these higher-value advisory offerings.

Skyrocketing Financial Performance

Median Net Client Fees (NCF) Evolution

Median Net Client Fees (NCF) for CAS practices have shown substantial growth, reflecting expanding market demand and increasing practice scale. Both "All Respondents" and "Top Performers" demonstrate a strong upward trajectory.

The robust NCF figures, particularly in recent years, suggest CAS practices are successfully attracting larger clients or expanding service scopes, solidifying CAS as a core business unit.

Boosting Efficiency & Value

Median Net Client Fees Per Professional (NCFPP)

NCFPP, a critical indicator of productivity and profitability, has steadily increased. This highlights enhanced operational leverage and a strategic shift towards delivering higher-value services that command premium fees.

Growth in NCFPP is driven by technology adoption, process standardization, staff upskilling, and evolving pricing strategies, all contributing to greater efficiency and value delivery.

Scaling Operations: Growth in CAS Client Base

Median Number of CAS Clients Served

The capacity to serve a growing number of clients is a key indicator of CAS practice scalability and market penetration. This chart shows the evolution of the median number of CAS clients served per firm, for both All Respondents and Top Performers, highlighting the expansion of client portfolios over time.

The consistent increase in the median number of CAS clients, particularly the jump seen in 2024, reflects firms' enhanced ability to manage larger client loads. This is likely due to improved processes, technology adoption, and effective team structures. Top Performers consistently manage a larger client base, underscoring their operational maturity and efficiency in service delivery.

Strategic Evolution: Foundational Shifts in CAS Practice Management

The CAS Benchmark Surveys reveal significant maturation in how firms approach CAS. Two key indicators of this strategic evolution are the shift in pricing methodologies towards value-based models and the increasing commitment to specialized teams. These trends highlight a move towards more sustainable, profitable, and expert-driven CAS practices.

Trend Towards Value: Adoption of Fixed-Fee Billing

This chart tracks the percentage of firms whose primary CAS billing method is "Fixed Fee/Recurring," indicating a move away from traditional hourly billing towards more predictable and value-aligned pricing.

Focus on Expertise: Trend in Dedicated CAS Staffing

This chart shows the percentage of firms reporting staff dedicated exclusively to CAS, a crucial element for developing deep expertise and efficient service delivery.

The increasing adoption of fixed-fee, recurring billing models demonstrates a growing understanding of value-based pricing and the desire for more predictable revenue streams in CAS. Similarly, the rise in dedicated CAS staffing underscores the recognition that specialized knowledge and focused teams are essential for delivering high-quality advisory services and achieving operational excellence. These foundational shifts are critical enablers of the sustained growth and profitability observed in the CAS sector.

Moving Upmarket: Serving Larger, More Complex Clients

Median Annual Revenue of CAS Clients

A significant trend in CAS evolution is the increasing size of clients served, as indicated by their median annual revenue. This shift suggests CAS practices are developing the capabilities to handle more complex engagements and are increasingly trusted by larger businesses for strategic financial guidance.

The steady rise in the median revenue of CAS clients, especially for Top Performers, signifies a move towards higher-value engagements. Serving larger clients often translates to more sophisticated advisory needs, greater fee potential, and deeper, more strategic client relationships. This trend reflects the growing maturity and expertise within CAS practices.

The CAS service model has significantly matured, characterized by a move towards more sophisticated offerings, strategic pricing, and robust operational structures, reflecting an evolution from compliance support to strategic advisory.
